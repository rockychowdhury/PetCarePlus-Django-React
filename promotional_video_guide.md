# 🎬 PetCarePlus Promotional Video Guide

Creating a sleek, professional promotional video without prior video editing experience is absolutely possible. By focusing on smooth scrolling, clean zooms, and concise text captions without complex audio, you can create a highly engaging presentation.

## 🛠️ Step 1: Essential Free Tools

You don't need expensive software. Here is the perfect free stack for this specific project:

1. **Screen Recording:** 
   - **OBS Studio (Free/Open Source):** The best tool for high-quality, water-mark free recording.
   - **Alternative (Browser Extension):** **Loom** or **Screenity** (Chrome extension) if OBS is too complex.
2. **Video Editing & Text/Captions:** 
   - **CapCut (Desktop or Web):** This is the ultimate free tool for beginners. It has amazing pre-built text animations, auto-captions, and extremely smooth zoom effects (called "Keyframes").
   - **Canva (Web):** Great for creating the opening/closing title slides and text overlays.

## 📸 Step 2: The Shot List (What & How to Capture)

Before you record, prepare your browser. Hide your bookmarks bar, go full-screen (F11), and ensure the app is running smoothly locally. 

Record these clips separately so they are easy to edit together:

### 1. The Opening Hook (Full Screen)
*   **Action:** Start at the very top of the homepage. Smoothly scroll down the entire page.
*   **Technique:** Don't use the scroll wheel! Click the scrollbar on the right and drag it down very slowly and smoothly, or use the down-arrow key for a consistent speed.
*   **Duration:** ~10-15 seconds.

### 2. Feature: AI Diagnostic Assistant (Zoomed)
*   **Action:** Open the AI Assistant. Select an animal, type a symptom, and hit submit. Show the chat interface generating text, and then the final report.
*   **Technique:** Record the full screen, but **we will zoom in during editing**. Make sure your mouse movements are deliberate and not jerky.
*   **Duration:** ~15-20 seconds.

### 3. Feature: Smart Location-Based Providers (Full Screen to Zoom)
*   **Action:** Open the Providers page. Click "Use Current Location" on the modal. Show the local providers loading.
*   **Technique:** Start full screen to show the map/modal, then we will zoom into the "Nearest Recommended Providers" section during editing.
*   **Duration:** ~10 seconds.

### 4. Feature: AI Polished Rehoming Applications (Zoomed)
*   **Action:** Go to the Rehoming section. Type a basic application message, click the "AI Polish" button (sparkle icon), and show the text transforming into a professional application.
*   **Technique:** Keep the mouse still while the text is updating so the viewer focuses on the text changing.
*   **Duration:** ~10 seconds.

### 5. Feature: Multilingual Support (Full Screen)
*   **Action:** Click the language toggle (English to Bangla). Watch the UI instantly translate.
*   **Technique:** Record full screen.
*   **Duration:** ~5 seconds.

## ✂️ Step 3: Editing Workflow in CapCut (Free)

CapCut makes it incredibly easy to add zooms and text.

1. **Import your clips:** Drag all your recorded clips into the CapCut timeline.
2. **Trim the fat:** Cut out any mistakes, loading times, or jerky mouse movements. Keep only the smooth, deliberate actions.
3. **Adding "Smooth Zooms" (Keyframes):**
   * Select a clip (e.g., the AI Rehoming text box).
   * In CapCut, go to the top right panel -> **Video** -> **Basic**.
   * Click the small diamond icon `⬦` (Keyframe) next to "Scale" and "Position" at the start of the clip.
   * Move the timeline forward 1 second, then increase the "Scale" (Zoom in) and adjust the "Position" to center the text box. CapCut will automatically create a buttery-smooth zoom transition between those two points.
4. **Adding Text Captions:**
   * Go to the **Text** tab. Choose a clean, modern font (like *Inter*, *Montserrat*, or *Proxima Nova*).
   * Place the text in the center of the screen or the bottom third.
   * **Tip:** Add a slight drop shadow or a dark semi-transparent background box behind your text so it is readable regardless of what is happening in the video behind it.
5. **Transitions:** Keep them simple. Use a simple "Fade" or "Cross Dissolve" between major features. Avoid flashy/cheesy transitions.

## 💡 Pro-Tips for a Premium Feel
* **Cursor Smoothing:** If your mouse looks jittery, you can use a free tool called **Cursor Pro** (Mac) or **Mouse Pointer Highlight** (Windows) to make the cursor look like a smooth, professional presentation dot.
* **Pacing:** People lose attention quickly. Keep the entire video under **60 seconds**.
* **Exporting:** Export in `1080p` or `4K` at `60 FPS` (Frames Per Second) to capture the true buttery smoothness of the CSS smooth-scrolling we implemented!
